What is iPH?

The Interactive Presenter for Handhelds (iPH) is a distributed application which supports sharing and co-edition os slide presentations, and which runs on both Windows XP and Windows Mobile.

It requires one of the participants to assume the role of an instructor (or moderator). The instructor is the one who controls the presentation, showing each one of the slides, and asking the other participants to give a written, or drawn, contribution at particular points of the presentation. For example, the instructor may present a slide with a question that is to be answered by all participants. The iPH supports input both in form of digital ink or typed text, that are printed over the image of a slide.

It can be used for any PowerPoint presentation. However, before the presentation can be displayed and shared through the tool, it has to be converted by a support tool named iDeck. This tool processes a batch of PowerPoint slides and converts them to JPEG images and links them appropriately. Hence, the presentation's animations are not preserved during this conversion.

The iPH comes in the XP and Mobile versions, of which the most notable difference is the graphical user interface (GUI). In the XP version it is possible to simultaneously display the thumbnail-view and the slide-view. In the Mobile version, this views are split into two different screens. Due to the special form and the limited size of the handheld display, some of the buttons are also placed at different positions.

Moreover, iPH supports the dynamic activation/de-activation of some of its functions at any moment. In this case, the corresponding button (or option) on the GUI appears in gray or becomes invisible. This may be useful in three cases: (a) if the functionality of iPH needs to be shaped according to the availability of the local resources, e.g. little amount of remaining energy or memory, which is particularly common for handhelds, or (b) if the ability of a participant to interact and contribute should be constrained by the current quality of the wireless ink, her/his location, or other context information, and (c) if the participant's interaction capabilities are to be individually and remotely controlled by the instructor.

With this feature, iPH can be easily integrated with any context-provision middleware, allowing a wide range of possibilities to become context-aware. In our case, we integrate with our middleware MoCA, wich provides information regarding the execution context, and a user's symbolic location.

Implementation

The iPH system is built on the top of Compact Conference XP (CCXP), which is port of ConferenceXP framework to .NET Compact Framework (CF). The ConferenceXP platform is a middleware for multipoint communication and synchronization that enables researches to create distributed applications that take advantage of ConferenceXP technology as well as Tablet PCsand wireless networks while enabling them to develop collaborative tools and applications without having to deal with low level communication and conectivity control.
